## ‚ú®Abstract

We propose <LaTeX inline formula="\texttt{StochSync}" />, a method for generating images in arbitrary spaces‚Äîsuch as 360¬∞ panoramas or textures on 3D surfaces‚Äîusing a **pretrained image diffusion model**. The main challenge is bridging the gap between the 2D images understood by the diffusion model (instance space <LaTeX inline formula="\mathcal{X}" />) and the target space for image generation (canonical space <LaTeX inline formula="\mathcal{Z}" />). Unlike previous methods that struggle without strong conditioning or lack fine details, <LaTeX inline formula="\texttt{StochSync}" /> combines the strengths of Diffusion Synchronization and Score Distillation Sampling to perform effectively even with weak conditioning. Our experiments show that <LaTeX inline formula="\texttt{StochSync}" /> outperforms prior finetuning-based methods, especially in 360¬∞ panorama generation.

---


## üí°Overall Idea

Our approach combines the strengths of two existing methods:

**Diffusion Synchronization (DS)**: Excels in producing detailed images but struggles with coherence across views when instance and canonical spaces are not pixel-aligned, often requiring strong guidance like depth maps.

**Score Distillation Sampling (SDS)**: Achieves coherent results, but lacks fine details and high-quality textures.

We observed that the coherence in SDS comes from using **maximum stochasticity** in the denoising process‚Äîspecifically, setting the noise level <LaTeX inline formula="\sigma_t" /> to its highest value during each reverse diffusion step in DDIM.

By incorporating maximum stochasticity into the reverse diffusion process of DS, along with other methods to further enhance quality, **we achieve the coherence benefits of SDS while retaining the detailed quality of DS.**
